About

I like building things that feel reliable and honest—systems you can understand, operate, and trust. I started out wiring networks and troubleshooting protocols, and somewhere along the way fell in love with backend software: Python/Django, clean APIs, and the small details that make code readable years later. Today, I live at the intersection of software and infrastructure, where design choices echo in performance, resilience, and day‑two operations.

On the network side, I’ve planned and implemented secure, high‑availability environments—routing, segmentation, and fault tolerance that keep traffic flowing when the unexpected happens. That experience shapes how I write software: mindful of latency, observability, and graceful failure modes. I like designs that scale by being simple, not by being clever.

I’m comfortable around relational databases—schema design, indexes, and query tuning for pragmatic wins. I’m not a full‑time DBA, but I know my way around an RDBMS and enjoy the craft of making data models that are both practical and durable.

Outside of work, I’m an Amateur Radio enthusiast. There’s something grounding about signals, protocols, and the discipline of communicating under constraints. It keeps me curious, and it reminds me that technology, at its best, is about clarity and connection.
